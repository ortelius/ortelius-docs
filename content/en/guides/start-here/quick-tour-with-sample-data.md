You don't need to connect your own repositories to see Ortelius working. Both the hosted and self-hosted experiences give you real data to explore immediately after account creation.

## Sample data out of the box

After you activate your account, you land on the **Welcome** page. It automatically adds a few popular public repositories to your Favorites, so there's real vulnerability, SBOM, and deployment data to explore — no action needed on your part for this step.

From there you can:

- Browse the **Organizations** view for a portfolio-level security overview
- Open the **Dashboard** to see the full executive summary, severity breakdown, and vulnerability trend for the sample repos
- Drill into individual **Vulnerabilities**, **Releases**, and **Endpoints**

## Favoriting additional public repos

You can also search for and favorite any other public GitHub or GitLab repository by name or `owner/repo`, even before connecting your own GitHub account. This is a good way to explore how Ortelius represents a project you already know well.

## When you're ready for your own data

Once you're comfortable navigating the dashboard, move on to [Understand How Ortelius Gets Data](../understand-how-ortelius-gets-data/) to connect your own GitHub repositories and start tracking your own software.
